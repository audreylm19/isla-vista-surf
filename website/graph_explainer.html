Graph Explainer

Colors

I chose the same color scheme that Surfline uses to distinguish conditions. Gray represents a score of zero, blue a score of one, green a score of two, orange a score of three, and red a score of four. An hour’s total score is the number of ideal wind, tide, period, and swell conditions met according to the following metrics:

  • If either of the below conditions are met, an hour’s wind is considered ideal
    • Any wind speed between 0mph and 4mph
    • Wind speed between 5mph and 10mph if wind direction is between 0 and 57 degrees
  • If both of the below conditions are met, an hour’s swell is considered ideal
    • Swell direction between 258 and 304 degrees
    • Swell Height between 1m and 3m
  • Tide between 2ft and 5ft is considered ideal
  • Period over 8 seconds is considered ideal

Hours without sufficient data are colored yellow.

Yearly Stacked Bar Graph

Each bar represents the total daylight hours in a year separated by score. Eventually, clicking on a bar will lead you to that year’s corresponding hover graph, but for now you’ll just see the total hours in each color block.

Monthly Hover Graph

Each brick you see represents a single daylight hour, and hovering over a brick will show that hour’s exact wind, swell, tide, and period.

This bar graph is actually a scatter plot in disguise—in order to preserve each hour’s conditions I manually assigned every hour an x-y position to form a bar graph. For this reason, I’ve omitted the y-axis, since the area of each color block represents a score’s total hours rather than the block’s height. You can see this area next to “total hours with this score” in the hover display.

To avoid seeing the information for multiple hours at once, you can click and drag to zoom in on a particular section of the plot.
